  The Federal Integrated Business Framework is a model that enables the Federal government to better coordinate and document common business needs across agencies and focus on outcomes, data, processes and performance. It is the essential first step towards standards that will drive economies of scale and leverage the government’s buying power.

Federal Business Lifecycle - Travel
Federal Business Lifecycles, service areas, functions, and activities serve as the basis for a common understanding of what services agencies need and solutions should offer.

Functions: Breakdown of a service area into categories of services provided to customers.

Activities: Within a function, processes that provide identifiable outputs/outcomes to customers are defined as activities.
Select from the list of available functions to view associated activities
  • Identifier Activity Description
    TRT.010.010 Travel Personnel Profile Set-up and Maintenance Document and maintain government/non-government travel personnel profile (e.g., name, agency, email address, travel preferences, frequent traveler information, travel card account); Determine and document Authorizing Official(s) and funds certifying officer; Determine family member eligibility for entitlement travel; Evaluate traveler special needs and document accommodation
    TRT.010.020 Travel Reservation Assistance and Processing Document traveler destination and travel dates; Identify transportation carrier, lodging and rental car options; Compare costs for itineraries; Select transportation carrier, lodging and rental car reservations and provide itinerary; Conduct travel reservation policy compliance checks and correct errors; Notify traveler of transportation carrier schedule change; Change travel reservations and provide updated trip itinerary; Cancel travel reservations; Receive and process traveler reservation assistance requests.
    TRT.010.030 Temporary Duty (TDY) and Local Travel Authorization Processing Determine and document estimated per diem; Determine and document estimated expenses for use with actuals for lodging, Meals and Incidental Expenses (M&IE), and subsistence; Determine and document estimated Privately Owned Vehicle (POV) expenses; Receive, evaluate and approve payment of expenses and/or payment in kind from non-Federal source(s); Develop and submit travel authorization and amendments; Conduct policy compliance checks of travel authorization or amendments and notify traveler of errors; Determine dollar amounts for each planned accounting code; Route, review and approve travel authorization; Verify availability of funds; Request and confirm obligation and deobligation of funds; Record final authorization for ticketing; Process travel advance/supplemental advance payment and document disbursement confirmation; Cancel travel authorization
    TRT.010.040 Travel Ticketing Issue and/or cancel traveler tickets; Conduct ticketing information compliance checks and identify errors; Identify and document credit(s) for unused ticket(s); Request and receive ticket refunds from transportation carrier
    TRT.010.050 Traveler Emergency Assistance Request Processing Evaluate and provide instructions for traveler emergency; Receive traveler/family member death notification and provide instructions; Evaluate and document expansion of family definition for emergency travel purposes
    TRT.010.060 Temporary Duty (TDY) and Local Travel Voucher Processing and Audit Determine and document per diem; Determine and document actual expenses for lodging (e.g., conventional, government quarters, lodging with friends), Meals and Incidental Expenses (M&IE), and subsistence; Determine and document Privately Owned Vehicle (POV) expense; Document miscellaneous and other expenses; Determine and document exchange rate adjustments for conversion of expenses paid in foreign currency; Document travel advance, denied boarding compensation, and expenses paid from non-Federal source; Develop and submit travel voucher or amended voucher and supporting documentation; Evaluate and document variances between estimated and incurred expenses; Evaluate incurred costs and constructive costs for transportation and per diem and document reimbursable cost; Conduct policy compliance checks of voucher or amended voucher; Document and notify traveler concerning disallowed travel expenses; Route, review and approve voucher; Verify availability of funds; Request and confirm obligation of funds; Request and confirm voucher or amended voucher payment; Process payment to charge card company for delinquent amount and provide payroll offset; Identify completed trip(s) with outstanding travel voucher(s)/advance(s) and notify traveler; Retain voucher records
    TRT.010.070 Temporary Duty (TDY) and Local Travel Monitoring and Reconciliation Monitor status of, reconcile and generate notices for unused travel advances and outstanding vouchers; Identify and monitor unused tickets and outstanding ticket credits/refunds owed by carrier for unfurnished services or portion of unused ticket; Determine penalty for accepting unauthorized payment for expenses from a non-Federal source; Reconcile agency centrally billed accounts and travel vouchers; Identify delinquent travel advances for payroll offset or collection
    TRT.010.080 Temporary Duty (TDY) and Local Travel Regulatory Reporting Provide required travel-related reports in accordance with law or regulation, including Travel Reporting Information Profile (TRIP) Report, Premium Class Travel Report (PCTR), expenses paid by non-Federal sources, and estimated improper travel payments
    TRT.010.090 Temporary Duty (TDY) and Local Travel Management Reporting and Analysis Provide travel reservation, authorization, ticketing and voucher information for agency and government-wide reporting and analysis; Identify trends and patterns in travel information; Provide TDY and local travel information for other agency purposes
    TRT.010.100 Temporary Duty (TDY) and Local Travel Policy Management and Audit Document per diem and mileage rates; Develop and document government-wide and agency policies and associated compliance checks; Document sample of travel documents (e.g., authorization, voucher, ticket) for audit; Conduct post-payment and travel policy/procedure compliance audits to validate information and expenses; Identify and document improper travel payments
